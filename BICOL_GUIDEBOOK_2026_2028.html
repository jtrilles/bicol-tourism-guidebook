The plan addresses seven critical gaps: foreign tourist share (1.2% vs 8% PH average), zero international flights, no digital presence beyond Facebook, severe provincial imbalance, low overnight ratios, no MICE infrastructure, and no crisis management framework.

2. Regional Tourism Vision 2026-2028

Vision: A unified, sustainable, and globally recognized tourism destination that positions the Bicol Region as a premier travel experience in the Philippines.

Mission: To develop, connect, and promote Bicol's diverse tourism assets through coordinated investment, community empowerment, environmental stewardship, strategic marketing, and world-class quality standards.

4. Theory of Change

IF we invest in strategic marketing and digital transformation, AND develop province-specific products addressing infrastructure gaps, AND implement quality standards and sustainability frameworks, AND secure international air access, AND build capacity, THEN Bicol will achieve 8.5M arrivals and PHP 34.5B revenue by 2028, BECAUSE the region possesses world-class assets that are currently under-marketed rather than inferior to competitors.

PART IV
Product and Market Development
10 niche tourism products.

20. Tourism Circuits

Four circuits: Mayon Circuit (2-3 days, volcano/food), Surf Circuit (5-7 days, surf/beach), Whale Trail (4-5 days, eco/dive), Faith and Culture Circuit (5-6 days, pilgrimage/heritage). Each circuit packages existing and planned products into marketable multi-day itineraries.

21. Food Tourism

Philippines named Michelin Most Exciting Food Destination 2026. Bicol's cuisine is distinctive. Strategy: Bicol Food Trail (20+ restaurants), cooking schools, Pili product innovation, food festivals. Target: 500K food tourists by 2028.

22. Dive Tourism

Primary asset: Ticao Island Manta Bowl. Strategy: Manta Ray Sanctuary, Dive Festival, mooring buoys, dive shop development. Target: 50K dive tourists by 2028.

23. Surf Tourism

Catanduanes Puraran Beach Baras Bowl rivals Siargao. Strategy: UPSA-sanctioned International Surf Cup, TESDA instructor certification, surf camp accommodation. Target: 30K surf tourists by 2028.

24. Faith Tourism

Penafrancia Festival: 1M+ attendees. 15+ Spanish-era churches. Strategy: Pilgrim services, digital queuing, heritage church trail across 6 provinces.

25. Festival and Events Tourism

Annual calendar across 6 provinces. Penafrancia (September) is largest. Strategy: Year-round content series, event marketing support, safety standards standardization.

26. Adventure Tourism

Products: Mayon ATV, CWC wakeboarding, Mt. Isarog trekking. Strategy: Safety certification, international competitions, trail development and maintenance.

27. Wellness Tourism

Assets: Tiwi Hot Springs, Bulusan hot springs, organic cuisine. Strategy: Wellness circuit development, hot spring resort upgrades, yoga and nature therapy packages.

28. MICE Tourism

No dedicated convention center currently. Strategy: Albay MICE Center development (PHP 50M), business hotel incentives, Bicol Meetings brand creation.

29. Community-Based Tourism

Approximately 5 informal CBTs, none formally certified. Strategy: CBT certification (ASEAN-aligned), comprehensive training, infrastructure support, marketing integration. Target: 20 certified CBTs by 2028.
